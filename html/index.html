Uniswap v4, What’s new?

Ethan Rouimi

Published in

CoinsBench

·
5 min read
·

In this article, we will delve into the highly anticipated update of Uniswap, the leading decentralized finance (DeFi) protocol on the Ethereum blockchain. 🦄 Uniswap v4, brings forth a range of significant changes that enhance customizability, gas efficiency, and overall user experience. With these updates, Uniswap continues to solidify its position as a trailblazer in the world of decentralized exchanges. Let’s explore the key features and improvements offered by Uniswap v4.

Let’s dive into what’s new in this update:

Hooks:

Uniswap v4 introduces a powerful feature called “Hooks”, which is determined when creating a new pool. Hooks are externally deployed contracts that execute developer-defined logic at specific points in a pool’s execution, this implementation opens a world of unlimited possibilities, changing the pool’s fees in a moment of volatility or incentivizing the trade of the pair, and also gives the capabilities of limit orders on-chain, and independent custom oracles, and more. These are the current callbacks that are called in the hook contract:

  • Before / After initialization of the pool
  • Before / After a change of position
  • Before / After a swap
  • Before / After a donate() (a new function, we will talk about this later)

Singleton Model:

Uniswap v4 adopts a singleton model, meaning that all the logic of the protocol is within one smart contract, departing from the model v3 that worked with a factory model. It worked in the way that each time someone wanted to create a token pair, it had to create a separate smart contract. In this version, there’s no need! all LP’s balances are managed by a single contract, the Uniswap-v4 core contract. This architectural shift simplifies the process operations 💫 with multiple LPs and greatly helps in gas savings for developers and users.

Flash Accounting: Efficient and Solvent Pools:

Flash accounting is a critical feature introduced in Uniswap v4 that significantly enhances the efficiency and cost-effectiveness of pool operations. In previous versions of the Uniswap Protocol, each operation, such as swapping or adding liquidity 🌊 to a pool, involved the direct transfer of tokens. However, Uniswap v4 introduces a new approach called flash accounting, which revolutionizes the way these operations are handled.

In Uniswap v4, instead of immediately transferring tokens, each operation updates an internal net balance known as a delta. This means that during the operation, the tokens are not immediately moved between accounts. Instead, the changes are recorded in the internal balance of the pool. The actual token transfers only occur at the end of the operation, known as the lock. This unique approach ensures that no tokens are owed to the pool manager or the caller by the end of the transaction, thus enforcing the pool’s solvency.

Flash accounting simplifies complex pool operations, including atomic swapping and adding liquidity 💰. By deferring the actual token transfers until the end of the operation, the protocol reduces the number of external transactions required, resulting in improved efficiency and reduced gas costs. This is particularly advantageous when combined with the singleton design pattern, which manages all pools with a single contract.

It is important to note that while flash accounting brings significant benefits, there are certain considerations. In the current execution environment, flash accounting can be costly due to the requirement of storage updates at every balance change. 🔒⚙️ Although the contract ensures that internal accounting data is not serialized to storage, users may still incur these costs once the storage refund cap is exceeded. However, in balancing this trade-off, Uniswap v4 leverages transient storage, as specified by EIP-1153 (which refunds the gas if storage slots are set to their original value before the end of the transaction), to implement efficient accounting for balances, ensuring that the balances are set to zero by the end of the transaction.

Native ETH Support:

Uniswap v4 goes to it’s roots! 🌱 bringing back support of Native Ether in its pairs, unlike v1 which supported only native Ether, and v2 which supported exclusively wrapped Ether. This version facilitates the option of having both ETH and WETH pairs, no more unnecessary transactions to wrap tokens! 🙌

Other Notable Features:

Uniswap v4 introduces additional notable features to enhance the overall protocol:

  • ERC1155 Accounting: Uniswap v4 supports the minting and burning of singleton-implemented ERC-1155 tokens for improved token accounting. This feature benefits frequent swappers and liquidity providers who continually use the same tokens across multiple blocks or transactions. 🔄💼
  • Governance Updates: Uniswap v4 introduces separate governance fee mechanisms for swap fees and withdrawal fees. Governance can now elect to take a capped percentage of swap fees and, if enabled by hooks, a capped percentage of withdrawal fees. 👥🗳️
  • Gas Reductions: Uniswap v4 implements gas optimizations through flash accounting, the singleton model, and native ETH support. The need for the protocol-enshrined price oracle, present in previous versions, is eliminated, resulting in potential gas savings on the first swap in each block, and making a pool deployment 99% cheaper. ⛽📉
  • donate(): The introduction of the donate() function enables users, integrators, and hooks to directly compensate liquidity providers in either or both tokens of the pool. This functionality expands the possibilities for new fee systems and tipping liquidity providers. 💰🎁

Uniswap is paving the way for a brighter future in decentralized exchanges, where innovation knows no bounds. As the DeFi landscape evolves, Uniswap remains a shining star, driving progress and shaping the future of decentralized trading. ✨
